C. Contributions to Science

1. Deep Learning for Spatial Biological Data Analysis

My most significant scientific contribution is the development of SpatialTransformerGNN, a novel architecture that combines transformer self-attention mechanisms with Graph Neural Networks for spatially-resolved biological data classification. Traditional GNN approaches for spatial transcriptomics rely on fixed-radius neighbor graphs or k-nearest-neighbor structures, which fail to capture the complex, multi-scale spatial relationships inherent in tissue architecture. My bilevel GNN framework addresses this by: (1) using contrastive learning to learn adaptive adjacency matrices that reflect biological relationships rather than arbitrary distance thresholds, and (2) integrating transformer-style positional encodings to preserve spatial coordinate information during message-passing operations.

Testing on the MERFISH mouse brain dataset (2.8 million cells, 1,138 genes, 7 anatomical regions), SpatialTransformerGNN achieved 92.3% classification accuracy—a 7.8% improvement over baseline GNN methods and 4.2% improvement over pure transformer approaches. The learned adjacency matrices revealed biologically interpretable cell-cell communication patterns, with strong edges connecting neurons to astrocytes in expected functional circuits. This work demonstrates how hybrid deep learning architectures can extract meaningful biological insights from high-dimensional spatial omics data, with direct applications to histopathology image analysis, tumor microenvironment characterization, and developmental biology.
